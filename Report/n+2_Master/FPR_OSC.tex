\begin{abstract}
During the current surge of research towards new ways of harnessing energy, the study of the performance of solar cells based on  organic materials is one of the most promising areas at the forefront. This is both due to their potentially cheap and easily up scaled production, as well as their small size when compared to inorganic solar cells. By using a spin coating and annealing process, we built five sets of organic solar cells with the P3HT:PCBM architecture and characterized them by analyzing their $I-V$ curves.
\end{abstract}
